GENERAL NOTES
1.Notify Hydro-Québec prior to activating the PV system. Net metering application must be approved before grid connection.
2.All electrical equipment shall be CSA-listed and installed per manufacturer's installation instructions.
3.Installer shall verify all dimensions and conditions at the job site. Report discrepancies to designer before proceeding.
4.All system components not specified herein shall be reviewed and approved by the manufacturer's representative.
5.All work shall be performed by or under the direct supervision of a licensed electrician (CMEQ/AECQ, Québec).
6.Installer shall verify adequate roof access pathways per NBCC and AHJ requirements before beginning work.
7.Installer shall verify structural integrity of roof rafters/trusses prior to installing the racking system.
8.Lag screws used for racking attachment shall penetrate a minimum of 63 mm (2½") into solid wood framing members.
9.Roof access pathways (ridge, eave, and ventilation access) shall be maintained per IFC Section 605.11.
10.All DC conductors shall be contained in conduit unless listed as PV wire or RW90-XLPE rated for sunlight exposure.
11.All plumbing vents, stacks, and HVAC equipment shall maintain required clearances from the PV system.
12.All junction boxes and wiring devices shall remain accessible without removing permanent construction per CEC Rule 12-3036.
13.Racking system shall be bonded to the grounding electrode system per CEC Rule 64-104.
14.PV module frames shall be bonded using WEEB (Washer/Lug Bonding) clip or equivalent listed bonding hardware.
15.All inverters shall be CSA C22.2 No.107.1 listed and certified for grid-interactive (GTI) operation.
16.Racking system shall be UL 2703 listed or carry equivalent Canadian certification per manufacturer specifications.
17.A grounding electrode shall be provided per CEC Rule 64-104 and interconnected with the utility grounding system.
ELECTRICAL NOTES
1.All conductors shall be copper unless otherwise noted. Aluminum conductors are not permitted for branch circuits.
2.PV source/output circuit conductors shall be identified as RW90-XLPE, PV wire, or USE-2 rated for sunlight resistance and wet locations.
3.All DC conductors shall be labeled/identified at every junction, pull box, and termination point per CEC Rule 64-214.
4.Conductors in exterior exposed locations shall be run in EMT or Schedule-40 PVC conduit per CSA C22.2 No.211.2.
5.All metallic raceways and equipment enclosures shall be bonded to the grounding electrode system.
6.Conduit fill shall not exceed 40% of interior cross-sectional area per CEC Table 6.
7.Inverter AC output grounding bond shall be maintained even when the inverter is removed from service.
8.Ground fault protection (GFP) is required for all grounded DC PV systems per CEC Rule 64-218.
9.GFCI protection and rapid shutdown shall be provided per CEC Rule 64-218(8) and AHJ requirements.
10.PV module frame bonding shall use listed bonding hardware (WEEB clip or equivalent) per CEC Rule 64-104.
11.Racking rail-to-rail bonding shall be maintained using listed splice connectors or WEEB bonding hardware.
12.Inverter shall be CSA C22.2 No.107.1 listed and approved for grid-interactive operation per Hydro-Québec standards.
13.Racking system shall be rated for design wind and snow loads per NBCC Part 4 Structural Design requirements.
14.A continuous grounding path shall be maintained from all module frames to the grounding electrode conductor.
15.All bus bar splices shall be rated for the conditions of use per applicable CEC requirements.
16.The PV backfed breaker shall be positioned at the opposite end of the bus bar from the main breaker per CEC Rule 64-404.
17.120% Rule: Sum of PV breaker + main breaker ampere ratings shall not exceed 120% of bus bar rating per CEC Rule 64-404.
